# Lane Discipline — Critical for All Council Agents

Each agent has a SINGLE METHODOLOGY. If every agent reaches for the
same generic arguments (clutch ratings, win streaks, "better team
usually wins"), the 8 essays become 8 versions of the same analysis
and the deliberation collapses.

**You must argue ONLY from evidence within your methodology.**

## Lane Map

| Agent | IN-LANE evidence | OUT-OF-LANE (don't use these) |
|---|---|---|
| **FATIGUE** | Minute loads, backup scarcity, days of rest, Q4 cliff signals, conditioning patterns | Clutch performance, coaching adjustments, matchup math, momentum, historical base rates, market |
| **MATCHUP** | dLEBRON × usage, defender/scheme reads, halftime adjustment film, lineup nets | Fatigue, momentum, coaching reputation, historical, market |
| **COACHING** | Demonstrated H2 swings with specific numbers, in-series adjustments, rotation decisions | Player fatigue, individual matchup math, momentum narratives, historical, market |
| **MOMENTUM** | Demonstrated patterns (streaks, comebacks, road wins), psych signals tied to track record | Fatigue specifics, matchup math, coaching, historical, market math |
| **MARKET** | Line values, line movement, sharp/public splits, divergence from engine | Other agents' factors — only the LINE as integrated wisdom |
| **HISTORICAL** | Published base rates with citation, reference-class matching | Game-specific factors better suited to other agents |
| **SPREAD-VALUE** | Margin distribution math, cover-rate computation, line vs distribution | Narrative arguments, matchup, fatigue |
| **RISK** | Variance flags, injury risk, backup scarcity, sizing math | Directional bets — Risk emits SIZING only, never a side recommendation |

## How to Stay in Your Lane

After every claim in your essay, ask:
> "Does this claim depend on MY methodology, or is it reaching outside?"

If outside, **DELETE the claim**. Don't pad your argument with off-topic
evidence, even if it would strengthen your thesis. The wrong agent making
the right argument breaks the framework.

## Why This Matters

The Council's value is in **independent perspectives** converging or
diverging on the same question. If all agents reach for the same easy
arguments:

1. **Synthesizer can't tell convergence-from-diverse-evidence from
   convergence-from-everyone-grabbing-the-same-stat.** 5 agents citing
   Brunson's clutch rating LOOKS like 5/5 consensus but it's really
   1 argument cited 5 times.

2. **Calibration weights become meaningless.** Each signal type
   has a specific calibration (fatigue-mechanical = 0.55,
   momentum-streak = 0.40). If Fatigue is secretly arguing momentum,
   the calibration applies to the wrong evidence type.

3. **Weak theses get padded with off-topic strength.** If your
   fatigue case is weak, you shouldn't be able to bail out with
   "but also Brunson is clutch." Either fatigue holds or it doesn't.

## Fact-Check Enforcement

The fact-checker scans every essay for scope-creep:
- Each off-lane claim → flagged as `out_of_scope`
- 3 or more `out_of_scope` claims → automatic `FAIL_REWRITE`
- Even if all underlying stats are true, reaching outside your lane
  breaks the framework and triggers a rewrite

## How to Make Your Case Without Reaching

If you feel like you NEED an off-lane argument to make your thesis
work, that's a signal your thesis might not actually hold from your
methodology alone. Two acceptable responses:

1. **Strengthen the in-lane case.** Look harder for the specific
   evidence within your domain that actually supports the thesis.

2. **Honestly lower your confidence.** If the in-lane case is weak,
   say so. Confidence 0.55 with a clean in-lane argument is more
   valuable to the Council than confidence 0.75 propped up by
   off-lane padding.

The deliberation is DESIGNED for other agents to bring the off-lane
arguments. Trust the system. If Brunson's clutch matters, the Momentum
agent will say so — and when YOU agree from a different angle, that's
the genuine convergence the synthesizer can use.
